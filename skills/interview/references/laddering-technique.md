# Laddering Technique Reference (with MI + ACT)

## Integration Summary
Laddering discovers value hierarchy by moving from surface preference to deep motive.
Polysona uses an integrated mode:
- **Laddering** for hierarchical "why" ascent,
- **MI (Motivational Interviewing)** for non-defensive elicitation,
- **ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy)** for value-aligned action under discomfort.

This integration prevents shallow preference capture and supports extraction of durable decision logic.

## Core Ladder Structure
Start from a concrete preference or behavior, then ascend:
1. **Surface preference** (what is chosen)
2. **Instrumental value** (what that enables)
3. **Terminal value** (why that ultimately matters)

Example:
- "I prioritize speed" -> "to capture timing" -> "to preserve agency and avoid regret"

## MI Layer (How to Ask)
- Use open questions, affirmations, reflective listening, and summaries.
- Avoid accusatory "why" repetition tone.
- Emphasize autonomy: the interviewee is the authority on meaning.

## ACT Layer (How to Ground)
- Identify values independent of emotional weather.
- Distinguish pain-avoidance from value-pursuit.
- Ask for committed action despite discomfort.

## 5 Specific Question Patterns
1. "You said X matters. What does X make possible that matters even more?"
2. "If X were fully true in your life, what kind of person would you be becoming?"
3. "When pursuing X gets hard, what inner cost are you willing to carry for it?"
4. "What fear appears when you try to live this value, and what action still fits the value anyway?"
5. "If we strip status, approval, and comparison away, why would this still matter to you?"

## Logging Pattern
Record each ladder as a short chain in interview-log:
- `~YYYY-MM-DD: Laddering (+MI+ACT): surface -> instrumental -> terminal`

## Output Mapping
- Primary destination: `persona.md decide (priority, approach)`
- Secondary evidence for `energy` and `risk` interpretation in later structuring.

## Cautions
- Stop ascent when terminal value stabilizes; do not badger.
- Do not confuse socially acceptable language with true motive.
- Validate with behavior examples, not words alone.
- Separate "ideal self talk" from "actual repeated choice pattern" and tag GAP when misaligned.
