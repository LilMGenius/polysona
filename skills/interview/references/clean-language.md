# Clean Language Reference

## Method Summary
Clean Language (David Grove) preserves the interviewee's metaphor space.
The interviewer follows the participant's exact words with minimal contamination.
This is critical for extracting unconscious structure without imposing interpretation.

## 12 Core Clean Language Questions (exact templates)
Use `[X]` for the interviewee's exact phrase and `[Y]` for a second exact phrase.

1. "And what kind of [X] is that [X]?"
2. "And is there anything else about [X]?"
3. "And where is [X]?"
4. "And whereabouts is [X]?"
5. "And that's [X] like what?"
6. "And is there a relationship between [X] and [Y]?"
7. "And when [X], what happens to [Y]?"
8. "And when [Y], what happens to [X]?"
9. "And what happens just before [X]?"
10. "And where could [X] come from?"
11. "And what would [X] like to have happen?"
12. "And what needs to happen for [X]?"

## Usage Protocol

### When to Use Metaphor Questions
- When participant uses image-rich language ("stuck", "exploding", "fog", "armor").
- When direct analytic questions produce rehearsed answers.
- When emotional tone shifts faster than explicit explanation.

### How to Follow Metaphor Chains
1. Capture the exact metaphor phrase.
2. Ask one clean question at a time.
3. Reuse participant wording exactly.
4. Track location, movement, relationship, and desired change in metaphor space.
5. Only transition to interpretation after the chain stabilizes.

### Depth Sequence Example
- Participant: "It feels like I'm carrying a heavy backpack."
- Interviewer: "And what kind of heavy backpack is that heavy backpack?"
- Interviewer: "And where is that heavy backpack?"
- Interviewer: "And what would that heavy backpack like to have happen?"

## Anti-Patterns (contaminating the metaphor space)
- "Is that backpack your childhood trauma?" (interviewer interpretation)
- "So you mean you're anxious, right?" (label insertion)
- "Wouldn't it be better to just let go?" (solution-leading)
- "Why are you like this?" (identity attack tone)
- Replacing client metaphor with interviewer metaphor (e.g., "backpack" -> "chains")

## Cautions
- Clean Language is extraction, not therapy or diagnosis.
- Respect silence; do not fill gaps with assumptions.
- Precision in wording is the method, not style preference.
