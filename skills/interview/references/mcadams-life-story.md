# McAdams Life Story Reference

## Model Summary
McAdams Life Story Interview treats identity as an internalized, evolving narrative.
The person is not reduced to traits; instead, meaning is traced through story structure:
- how life is segmented into chapters,
- how turning points are interpreted,
- and whether difficult experiences are integrated as growth (redemption) or decline (contamination).

For Polysona, this framework is foundational for `persona.md core` because it captures the long-arc identity logic behind decisions and behavior.

## Key Concept 1: Life Chapters
Life chapters reveal the narrative architecture a person uses to organize selfhood.
Different chapter boundaries indicate different identities (survivor, builder, explorer, teacher, etc.).

### 5 Specific Question Patterns (Life Chapters)
1. "If your life were a book with 5-7 chapters, what are the chapter titles and date ranges?"
2. "Which chapter still feels unfinished, and what makes it unfinished?"
3. "Which chapter do you rarely tell others about, and why?"
4. "Which chapter gave you your strongest operating rule for work or relationships?"
5. "If your current chapter had a subtitle, what would it be and what conflict defines it?"

## Key Concept 2: Turning Points
Turning points are identity inflection nodes where meaning, direction, or role changed.
The event itself matters less than interpretation and aftermath.

### 5 Specific Question Patterns (Turning Points)
1. "Describe one high point, one low point, and one turning point; what changed after each?"
2. "What decision at that turning point still shapes your behavior today?"
3. "At that moment, what did you stop believing and what did you start believing?"
4. "Who was present at the turning point, and whose voice influenced your next move?"
5. "If that turning point had not happened, who would you likely be now?"

## Key Concept 3: Redemption vs Contamination Sequences
McAdams highlights two identity narratives:
- **Redemption sequence**: bad -> good transformation.
- **Contamination sequence**: good -> bad deterioration.
These sequences predict hope, resilience style, and threat perception.

### 5 Specific Question Patterns (Redemption/Contamination)
1. "Tell a story that began badly but became meaningful or beneficial over time."
2. "Tell a story that began well but later felt ruined; where did it turn?"
3. "When something fails, do you look first for lesson, blame, or retreat?"
4. "What repeated pattern turns stress into growth for you, if any?"
5. "What repeated pattern turns success into loss for you, if any?"

## Output Mapping Guidance
- Primary destination: `persona.md core (bio, narrative)`
- Log first as evidence lines in `persona.md interview-log`:
  - `~YYYY-MM-DD: McAdams Life Story: ...`

## Cautions
- Do not romanticize trauma as necessary growth.
- Do not force coherence where genuine contradiction exists.
- Keep timeline and interpretation separate (event vs meaning).
- Use participant words for emotionally salient scenes.
